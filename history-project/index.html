Emergency Relief and Construction Act

Preamble: During the Great Depression, the United States faced unprecedented economic challenges, with millions suffering from unemployment, poverty, and homelessness. Recognizing the urgent need for relief and recovery efforts, the government hereby enacts the Emergency Relief and Construction Act to provide immediate assistance to those in need and stimulate economic activity through infrastructure development.

The government shall allocate funds to establish relief programs aimed at providing direct assistance to individuals and families severely affected by the economic downturn. These relief programs shall include, but not be limited to, unemployment benefits, food assistance, housing subsidies, and healthcare services.

Congress shall authorize periodic reviews of funding requirements and may allocate additional resources as deemed necessary to sustain relief efforts and advance infrastructure development.

The Economic Recovery and Stability Act of 1931

The purpose of this Act is to address the economic challenges facing the United States during the ongoing Great Depression. It aims to stabilize financial markets, promote employment, and restore confidence in the nation's economy.

The NEC shall analyze economic data, assess policy options, and provide recommendations to the President and Congress on measures to alleviate the economic downturn. The NEC shall convene regular meetings and collaborate with relevant agencies to coordinate economic policies and initiatives.

The Federal Reserve is granted expanded authority to implement monetary policies aimed at stabilizing financial markets and promoting credit availability. The Federal Reserve shall have the power to conduct open market operations, adjust discount rates, and provide liquidity support to banks and financial institutions as deemed necessary to address economic instability.
